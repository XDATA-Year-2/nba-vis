  Notebook: Grizzlies 85, Bobcats 80 Posted Friday April 20, 2012 10:10PM By Derek S. Smith, for NBA.com THE FACTS:   Mike Conley  scored 20 points and  Zach Randolph  added 14 off the bench as the Memphis Grizzlies picked up an 85-80 come-from-behind win over the abysmal Charlotte Bobcats Friday night at Time Warner Cable Arena.  Gerald Henderson  led all scorers with a career-high 32 points for the Bobcats who have lost 19 straight. QUOTABLE:  "We just had guys that stepped things up defensively and the glass hard. Our pick and roll was really hitting it tonight. We found a group that really wanted it out there and just went with them. This was a little closer than we wanted, but we got the win."   -- Memphis guard  Mike Conley THE STAT:  Points off turnovers. The Bobcats committed 22 turnovers and allowed the Grizzlies to convert them into 27 points. Charlotte had seven turnovers in the final quarter which got turned into nine points. TURNING POINT:  Turnovers. Memphis went on an 8-0 with the score all tied at 71. The first six points were all of dunks including two from former Bobcats'  Dante Cunningham  as Memphis took the lead for good. Charlotte rookie  Kemba Walker  had two turnovers in that stretch that led directly to baskets. HOT:  Gerald Henderson scored a career-high 32 points for the Bobcats. The Charlotte shooting guard was 11-for-21 from the floor and a perfect 6-for-6 from the charity stripe. He added four assists and three rebounds on the night. It's his third 20-point game in the last four contests. He's averaging 22.8 points in that span. FANTASY SPOTLIGHT:  Mike Conley had a team-best 20 points and dished out seven assists while adding two rebounds and three steals. Memphis is now 8-0 when Conley scores 20 or more this season. He is averaging 18.7 points, 6.7 assists, 2.0 steals and 2.3 rebounds in the three-game span. INSIDE THE ARENA:  The Bobcats hosted Faith & Family Night at Time Warner Cable Arena with Christian recording artist Building 429 as the featured performer. ROOKIE WATCH:  Kemba Walker had 11 points, six rebounds and five assists in the loss for Charlotte. The rookie point guard is averaging 13.7 points, 4.8 assists and 4.2 rebounds in his last 12 games. He has scored in double figures in 10 of the last 12 games. Fellow rookie  Bismack Biyombo  netted seven points and grabbed 10 rebounds. NOTABLE:   Byron Mullens  had a double-double with 11 points and 11 rebounds. It was his sixth double-double of the year ...  Jamario Moon  is 0-for-7 since being signed by Charlotte earlier this week ... the Bobcats are now 6-3 when leading after three quarters .... Charlotte is 3-13 in games decided by six or less while Memphis is 18-8 in games decided by six or less ... The Grizzlies had 42 bench points compared to just eight for the Bobcats and scored 58 points in the paint ...  Lester Hudson  was signed earlier today by the Grizzlies, but was inactive for the game tonight ... this was the first win in Charlotte by the Grizzlies since March 10, 2007 ... Memphis is now 13 games over .500 for the first time since the end of the 2005-06 season ...  Rudy Gay  scored nine points and was held to single digits for just the fourth time this season. UP NEXT:  For the Grizzlies, Saturday vs. Portland, Monday vs. Cleveland, Thursday vs. Orlando. For the Bobcats, Sunday vs. Sacramento, Monday at Washington, Wednesday at Orlando.
