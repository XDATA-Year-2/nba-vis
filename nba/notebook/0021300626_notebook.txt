Notebook: Sixers 110, Knicks 106 
 
 
 Adam Zagoria, for NBA.com 
 
Posted Wed Jan 22, 2014 11:25 PM - Updated Wed Jan 22, 2014 11:25 PM 
 
 THE FACT:  The Knicks dropped to 7-15 at Madison Square Garden with their fifth straight loss and third in a row on the current homestand. 
 
 THE LEAD: Evan Turner  poured in a career-high 34 points on 13-for-22 shooting and grabbed 11 rebounds as the last-place Philadelphia 76ers snapped a three-game losing streak by beating the New York Knicks 110-106 at MSG Wednesday night. 
 The Sixers' leading scorer on the night, Turner had averaged just 9.7 points over his last three games.  Michael Carter-Williams  went for 19 points, 12 rebounds and seven assists for Philadelphia (14-28), while  Thaddeus Young  tallied 19 points and seven rebounds.  James Anderson  added 18 points and seven rebounds. 
 Losers of five straight, the Knicks (15-27) were led by  Carmelo Anthony 's 28 points and seven assists.  Andrea Bargnani  added 20 points,  Iman Shumpert  had 19 points and eight reboundsm while  J.R. Smith  went for 14 points off the bench.  Tyson Chandler  notched a double-double of 10 points and 14 boards. 
 
 QUOTABLE:  "It was great. I'll never forget the game. It was an unbelievable win as a team so I definitely won't forget it."  
 -- Philly's Michael Carter-Williams on his first pro game at  T he Garden 
 
 
 THE STAT:  The Sixers outrebounded the Knicks, 54-39, and outscored the Knicks 50-26 in the paint. 
 
 TURNING POINT:  After the Knicks opened the fourth quarter on a 10-0 run to take a 90-84 lead, the Sixers rattled off a 16-5 spurt to go up 100-95. With the Knicks trailing 102-100, Young later hit a clutch 3-pointer with 1:47 remaining and Turner added a jumper to put the Sixers up 107-100. 
 
 QUOTABLE II : "That's a big 3 that we give up with Thaddeus. It was a simple play but we just didn't execute it properly." 
 -- Knicks coach Mike Woodson 
 
 
 HOT:  Carter-Williams, a leading candidate for Rookie of the Year honors, posted a double-double in his first game as a pro at The Garden. He entered leading all rookies in points, rebounds, assists and steals. 
 
 NOT:  Knicks point guard  Raymond Felton  continued to struggle, going 4-for-10 for nine points and seven assists. He had combined to go 4-for-19 for 14 points in his previous two games. 
 
 QUOTABLE III:  "We gave up 110 points tonight, it's just too many."  
 -- Mike Woodson 
 
 
 QUOTABLE IV:  "We shared the ball and we executed down the stretch so it was great."  
 -- Michael Carter-Williams 
 
 
 NOTABLE:  Sixers coach  Brett Brown  said before the game it's possible  Nerlens Noel  might not play at all this season as he recovers from ACL surgery. "I'm really not sure," Brown said. "I mean, anything's possible. I mean at this stage, we're moving forward and he's doing a good job." ...The Knicks continued to play without  Amar'e Stoudemire  and  Kenyon Martin , both of whom have sprained left ankles. Martin said before the game he's feeling "good" and hoped to be back within the two-week timeframe, which would be late next week. ...Anthony and Chandler were named to the 28-man USA Basketball roster for the 2014 World Championships and 2016 Olympics. 
 
 UP NEXT:  For the Knicks, Friday vs. Charlotte, Sunday vs. L.A. Lakers, Tuesday vs. Boston For the Sixers, Friday vs. Toronto, Saturday vs. Oklahoma City, Monday vs. Phoenix.
