Notebook: Spurs 126, Nuggets 100 
 
 
 Andrew McNeill, for NBA.com 
 
Posted Sun Nov 18, 2012 12:15 AM - Updated Sun Nov 18, 2012 12:15 AM 
 
 THE FACTS:  The Spurs went on a 22-2 run in the first half to build an early lead over the Nuggets en route to a 126-100 win. Six Spurs hit double figures in scoring, lead by  Manu Ginobili  with 20 points off the bench.  DeJuan Blair  added 19 points and eight rebounds. 
 San Antionio forced the Nuggets into 22 turnovers on the night and outshot Denver in every phase of the game. 
 Denver was led by  Danilo Gallinari  with 15 points.  Kenneth Faried  added 14 points. 
 
 QUOTABLE:  "[The Spurs] did a great job of finding the open man; hitting somebody with a little bit better shot."  
 -- Spurs coach  Gregg Popovich 
 
 
 THE STAT:   San Antonio hit 16 3-pointers to the Nuggets' four. All but three players who saw action for the Spurs knocked down a shot from beyond the arc. 
 
 TURNING POINT:  The Spurs hit four 3-pointers in the first quarter, while Denver committed nine turnovers.  
 
 QUOTABLE II:  "I think San Antonio would've beat any team tonight by the way they played with their ball movement and shooting."  
 -- Nuggets coach  George Karl 
 
 
 HOT:  Ginobili. The Spurs' sixth man has struggled in the early season, but broke out against Denver with 20 points on 7-for-10 shooting and 5-for-7 from beyond the arc. 
 
 NOT:  Denver's shooters. The Nuggets' starters were 0-for-4 from 3-point range and 21-for-45 from the field. 
 
 FANTASY SPOTLIGHT:  In addition to his 20 points, Ginobili had five 3-pointers, four assists and a steal for San Antonio.  Tony Parker  had a line of 14 points, six assists, five rebounds and two steals.  Corey Brewer  had 14 points, two 3-pointers and seven rebounds. 
 
 ROOKIE WATCH:   Evan Fournier  had 10 quick points and two assists in seven minutes of late game action for the Nuggets.  Nando De Colo  gave San Antonio 17 solid minutes with five points and three assists.  Quincy Miller  was inactive for Denver. 
 
 NOTABLE:  Tim Duncan  hit a 3-point shot from the corner to end the first half, it was his first made 3-pointer of the season. San Antonio lead by as many as 32 points in this game. San Antonio had a season-high 33 assists against the Nuggets. 
 
 UP NEXT:  For the Nuggets, Monday @ Memphis, Wednesday @ Minnesota, Friday vs. Golden State. For the Spurs, Monday vs. L.A. Clippers, Wednesday @ Boston, Friday @ Indiana.
